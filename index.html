Discover the expertise of our Podiatrists

A podiatrist is a university-trained and registered healthcare professional, pivotal in the realm of allied health. Trained in diagnosing and managing disorders, injuries, and pain affecting the foot, ankle, and lower limbs, podiatrists play a crucial role in enhancing mobility and promoting longevity. As key members of the allied health workforce, podiatrists operate with an advanced scope of practice. They hold the unique ability to prescribe medicines as endorsed prescribers and perform intricate surgical procedures as podiatric surgeons. At Ryan James Podiatry our team comprises of an endorsed podiatrist and Podiatric surgeon so you can be confident all your foot and ankle concerns can be managed. ​

Shockwave Treatment

Shockwave therapy is a non-invasive way of treating chronic musculoskeletal injuries. At Ryan James Podiatry we are proud to be able to provide our patients with this innovative technology. Shockwave therapy promotes neovascularisation which promotes new circulation to the damaged tendon or fascia. During shockwave therapy a high intensity sound wave interacts with the tissues of the body, which promotes healing of the area. Benefits of Shockwave therapy:

  • No anaesthesia
  • No medication
  • High success rate
  • Non-invasive

Orthotic Therapy

Orthotic therapy is used to treat biomechanical foot dysfunction. Foot dysfunction is when the foot is not moving as it should. This can place extra stress to bone, ligament and muscles of the foot. Our Podiatrists may recommend some form of orthotics determined on a patients individual needs this may include a prefabricated device, a heat moulded prefabricated device or prescription orthotics. Our prescription orthotics are built to meet your individual biomechanical and lifestyle needs ultilising modern technologies with 3D casting and highly skilled laboratory technicians.

Children's Podiatry

Children's Podiatry Children’s feet are different in many ways to adults as they have unique developmental milestones and patterns. The following can be reasons why you may want to bring your child to a podiatrist:

  • recurring pain
  • frequent tripping
  • tiring easily
  • knocked knees
  • uneven shoe wear
  • flat feet
  • walking or standing differently
  • skin rashes
  • ingrown nails
  • intoeing (pigeon toes)
  • juvenile hallux abducto valgus (bunion deformity)

Diabetic Podiatry

Diabetes is one of the fastest growing diseases in Australia and is rapidly reaching epedemic proportions. Did you know that 60-70% of those with diabetes will develop peripheral neuropathy, or lose sensation in their feet.

Diabetic foot screening

Ryan James Podiatry has devised an evidence based screening tool to assess your risk of diabetic foot complications comprised of vascular assessment, neurological assessment and recording of deformity and pressure areas.

Fungal Nail Treatment

Fungal nails are a common concern for many patients and they are often a difficult nail condition to treat. Some symptoms may include discolouration, thickening, brittleness and debris under the nails. At Ryan James Podiatry we offer Photodynamic Therapy (PACT therapy).

Sports podiatry

Sports injuries to the foot and ankle are exceedingly common in sports. We understand that a sports specific approach is needed to assessing, treating and preventing injury. Some of the treatments for Sports Podiatry related concerns at our clinic include:

  • Biomechanics and gait analysis
  • Orthotic Therapy
  • Physical Therapy
  • Soft tissue and joint manipulation or mobilisation
  • Shockwave Therapy
  • Injection Therapies
  • Dry needling
  • Stretching programs
  • Kinetic or functional taping techniques
  • Electrophysical treatments
